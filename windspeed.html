Wind Speed

Wind speed describes how fast the air is moving past a certain point. This may be an average over a given unit of time, such as miles per hour, or an instantaneous speed, which is reported as a peak wind speed, wind gust or squall.

The wind speed does not appear to change significantly as one approaches the equator (0 Deg Latitude). However there a slight increase in wind speed in the southern hemisphere is noted. This may be due to the increased temperature, but more information is necessary to determine if this is true.
